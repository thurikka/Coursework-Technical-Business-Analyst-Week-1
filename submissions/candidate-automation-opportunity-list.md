# Automation Opportunities 

## High-volume, repeatable, rules-driven, lower-risk steps identified

- Worklist selection and prioritisation (high-volume, repeatable, rules-driven)
- Legacy account status checks (high-volume, repeatable, rules-driven)
- Spreadsheet updates and reconciliation (high-volume, repeatable, rules-driven)
- Customer contact attempts for standard cases (high-volume, repeatable)
- Follow-up scheduling for promise-to-pay and no-response cases (repeatable, rules-driven)
- Manager reporting and status reconciliation (high-volume, repeatable, rules-driven)



## Automation Opportunities:

### 1. Automated worklist prioritisation
Replace manual case selection with rule-based prioritisation using risk flags, days past due, and last contact date.

---

### 2. Unified account view
Combine legacy system, spreadsheet, and email data into a single consolidated account view to remove manual cross-checking.

---

### 3. Automated follow-up scheduling
Automatically generate and track follow-up dates based on outcomes such as promise-to-pay or no response.

---

### 4. Self-service customer portal (simple cases)
Enable customers to view balances, repayment options, and set up payment plans without agent involvement for low-risk accounts.

---

### 5. Real-time reporting dashboard
Replace manual reconciliation with live reporting of account status, follow-ups, and workload across systems.
