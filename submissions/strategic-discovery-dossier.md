# Discovery Brief 

One-page discovery brief for Legacy-Trust Bank.

## 1. Problem summary

Legacy-Trust’s debt recovery relies on fragmented, manual processes that struggle to scale. Over 100,000 delinquent accounts are handled by more than 50 agents using spreadsheets, email threads, and a 20-year-old collections database. This creates inconsistent workflows, heavy reliance on individual memory, and poor tracking. As a result, follow-ups are missed, efforts are duplicated, and there is limited visibility into account status, pending actions, or urgent cases. Growing account volumes further reduce productivity and delay recovery, contributing to an estimated 15% revenue loss. Leadership need clear evidence of process breakdowns, identification of automation opportunities, and a credible 12-month ROI with transparent assumptions to guide Phase 1 improvements, including a potential self-service recovery portal.

## 3. Discovery questions

- Which steps in debt recovery are high-volume and rules-driven enough for self-service?
- Where do spreadsheets and manual handoffs create duplicate work?
- Which baseline metrics best show operational waste and revenue leakage?

- How do agents currently decide which accounts need immediate attention versus which can wait?
- Which tasks do agents frequently have to redo or double-check because of unclear handoffs or missing information?
- Are there any routine decisions agents make that could be reliably automated without risking compliance or customer dissatisfaction?
- Which types of account/customer interactions contributes to lose revenue or agent workload and why?
- Which parts of the process are most stressful or confusing for the agents?

## 5. Final problem statement

Legacy-Trust’s debt recovery operations remain heavily manual, relying on spreadsheets, email trails, and a 20-year-old collections database to manage over 100,000 delinquent accounts. Agents spend significant time reconciling account status and avoiding duplicated or unnecessary customer contact, while managers lack clear visibility into follow-ups and performance. This fragmented approach leads to missed recoveries, inconsistent reporting, operational inefficiencies, and measurable revenue loss. Without a unified system, simple and complex cases are intertwined, limiting scalability and opportunities for automation. Legacy-Trust requires a clear understanding of current pain points, process bottlenecks, and where self-service and automation can improve recovery rates, reduce revenue loss, and drive more efficient operations.
